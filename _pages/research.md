## _Theme 1 - Human-Centered Autonomy_

*Designing autonomous systems that align with human behavior, cognition, and expectations.*

I develop human-centered autonomy frameworks that enable intelligent systems to interact safely, intuitively, and transparently with humans. The work integrates imitation learning, inverse reinforcement learning, and behavioral modeling to incorporate human reasoning and decision patterns into autonomous control. Using connected and autonomous vehicles as a testbed, the study proposes human-like control strategies that improve interaction quality, trust, and safety in mixed human-autonomy environments. This research advances autonomy that is not only effective in performance, but also interpretable and aligned with human expectations.

## _Theme 2 - AI-Enabled Control for Autonomous Systems_

*Learning-based control for adaptive decision-making in dynamic, multi-agent environments.*

I develop learning-based control frameworks that enable autonomous systems to operate robustly in dynamic and uncertain environments. The work focuses on integrating reinforcement learning with control principles to model interactions among multiple agents and adapt to evolving system conditions. Using connected and autonomous vehicles as a representative testbed, the study designs proactive control strategies that anticipate and influence surrounding behaviors, improving safety and system efficiency. This line of research establishes a foundation for scalable and adaptive autonomy in complex real-world systems.

## _Theme 3 - End-to-End Generative Autonomy_

*Leveraging vision-language-action models for unified perception, reasoning, and control.*

We develop end-to-end generative frameworks that integrate perception, reasoning, and action within a unified learning architecture. The work explores vision-language-action (VLA) models and large-scale generative AI to enable autonomous systems to directly map multimodal inputs, including visual observations, sensor data, and natural language instructions, into decision-making and control. Unlike traditional modular pipelines, these approaches reduce information loss across perception, reasoning, and control modules, and improve generalization in complex, long-tail scenarios. The study further focuses on designing efficient and deployable architectures that balance reasoning capability with real-time performance, advancing scalable autonomy in open-world environments.

## _Theme 4 - Human Behavior and Cognitive Modeling through Human-in-the-Loop Experiments_

*Understanding human behavior and cognition to enable effective human-autonomy interaction.*

We study how humans perceive, respond to, and interact with autonomous systems through human-in-the-loop experiments and data-driven analysis. The work combines survey data, behavioral data, physiological sensing (e.g., ECG, EEG), and simulation-based experimentation to model human decision-making, trust, evolution, and adaptation in dynamic environments. These insights are used to inform the design of human-centered autonomy, enabling systems to better anticipate human actions and respond in ways that are safe, intuitive, and effective.
